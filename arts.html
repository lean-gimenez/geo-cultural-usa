LITERATURE

First 200 years

  • Native American literatures: oral
  • It disappreared with white settlement
  • American prose themes: American colonies
  • American prose genres: stories, religious writings, expedition and travel narratives

After independence

  • Fiction appears
  • Novels about America's past
  • America's own voice
  • Edgar Allan Poe

XXth century

  • Communications revolution: motion pictures, radio & TV
  • Literary voices reached a national audience
  • Henry James, Jack London, H.P. Lovecraft

After WWI

  • Lost Generation
  • Bitternes about the war, loss of hope towards American society
  • Ernest Hemigway, William Faulkner, F. Scott Fitzgerald

After atomic bomb

  • Possibility of mass destruction
  • Diversification of perspective
  • Context became themes: civil rights movement, students movement, anti-Vietnam War movement, women's movement, gay rights movement, environmental movement
  • Non-fiction
  • Truman Capote, Norman Mailer

MUSIC

Blues & Ragtime

  • late XIXth century
  • African American performers
  • Ragtime derives from Blues
  • Sad stories about poor African Americans
  • Scott Joplin, Bessie Smith

Jazz

  • New Orleans
  • Sometimes improvised music with a lot of instruments.
  • Nostalgic topics
  • Louis Armstrong, Duke Ellington, Frank Sinatra

Folk music

  • Social and political commets related to working-class and rural lives
  • Vernon Dalhart, Jimmie Rogers

Country&Western

  • Southern music
  • Based on Folk songs from Scottish and English colonial settlers
  • Disadvantaged rural lives of poor whites
  • Vernon Dalhart, Jimmie Rogers

Musical

  • Early XXth century
  • Combination of acting, music and dancing
  • Fiction and non-fiction. Topics from all over the world
  • Influence of jazz
  • Oscar Hammerstein II, Marvin Hamlisch, Andrew Lloyd Webber, Jerome Kern, Bob Fosse

Rock n Roll

  • 1950s
  • Combination of African American Blues and White Country&Western
  • More speed to music
  • Elvis Presley, Bill Haley and His Comets, Creedence

MOTION PICTURES

First American movies

  • Early 1900s in New York & New Jersey
  • Novels about America's past
  • America's own voice
  • Charles Chaplin, Mary Pickford

Noir cinema

  • 1940s & 1950s
  • Dark & abstract scenery
  • Influenced by German Expressionism. Criticism to Materialism
  • Non-fiction
  • Fritz Lang, Hitchcock, Stanley Kubrick

Hollywood

  • US moviemakers gathered as a film community
  • South California
  • D.W. Griffith

Indpendent movies & Movie brats

  • 1960s & 1970s
  • Low budget. Improvisation and creativity
  • Martin Scorsese, Francis Ford Coppola, Steven Spielberg, George Lucas, Brian de Palma, Andy Warhol, Stanley Kubrick

Away from the cinema

  • Moviemakers who don't succeed at the box-office, get a better audience in domestic watching
  • VHS, DVDs. Extra scenes. Behind the scenes.
  • Quentin Tarantino, Paul Thomas Anderson

PAINTING

XVIIIth century

  • History painting & portraits
  • Self-taught painters
  • Joseph Badger, Robert Feke

XIXth century

  • Hudson River School (1820)
  • Rural US. Middle-class city life. Historical and military paintings
  • Thomas Cole, Thomas Eakins, William B. T. Trego

Early XXth century

  • Realism & Naturalism European styles
  • Adaptation to American themes & locations
  • Charles Chaplin, Mary Pickford

Post WWII

  • Abstract Expressionism
  • New York
  • Rejection to established painting styles and subject matters
  • Non-fiction
  • Jackson Pollock, Mark Rothko

Collage-type painting & PopArt

  • Ordinary everyday objects. Consumer society objects. Popular culture themes.
  • 1950s & 1960s
  • Roy Lichtenstein, Andy Warhol, Jasper Johns

Contemporary Art

  • Late XXth century - XXIst century
  • Anything goes
  • Geometric abstraction, Hyperrealism, Photorealism, Graffitti, Minimalism, Neo-Expressionism, Neo-Dada paintint, Collage, Op Art, Landscape painting
